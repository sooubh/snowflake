## 1. PUBLIC SUPPLY DEPOT (PSD) Perspective

### Who They Are
- **Government medical stores** at district, state, and central levels
- **Primary Health Centers (PHCs)** - 30,000+ across India
- **Community Health Centers (CHCs)** - 5,000+ facilities
- **Sub-centers** - 150,000+ last-mile delivery points

### Their World (Current State)

**A Day in the Life of a PSD Manager:**
```
6:00 AM - Arrive at office, check registers (paper-based)
8:00 AM - Receive delivery truck, manual counting, verification
10:00 AM - Process requests from 10 PHCs (phone calls, manual paperwork)
12:00 PM - Prepare monthly report for district collector (Excel, manual data entry)
2:00 PM - Stock audit surprise visit - scramble to find records
4:00 PM - Medicine expired last week discovered - write explanation
6:00 PM - Still processing paperwork, go home late
```

**Pain Points**:
1. **Accountability Pressure**
   - CAG audits are terrifying
   - Every medicine must be accounted for
   - One mistake = career damage

2. **Distribution Challenges**
   - 50+ delivery locations to track
   - No confirmation of receipt
   - "Where did 500 tablets go?" - impossible to answer

3. **Political Pressure**
   - Media exposes on wastage
   - Public complaints about stock-outs
   - Minister's office calls demanding answers

4. **Manual Hell**
   - 10 registers to maintain
   - Duplicate entries everywhere
   - Report generation takes 3-5 days

## 2. HOSPITAL Perspective

### Who They Are
- **Multi-specialty hospitals** (200+ beds)
- **Nursing homes** (50-150 beds)
- **Medical colleges** with attached hospitals
- **Specialty centers** (Cancer, Cardiac, Maternity)

### Their World (Current State)

**A Day in the Life of a Hospital Pharmacist:**
```

       - Rush to check manual register
```

**Pain Points**:
1. **Patient Safety Risk**

2. **Financial Hemorrhage**

   - Theft/pilferage (no tracking)

3. **Department Chaos**
   - Surgery has stock, ICU doesn't - poor coordination
   - Each department tracks separately
   - Duplicate purchases
   - Turf wars over supplies

4. **Compliance Nightmares**
   - NABH accreditation requires detailed records
   - Insurance claims need documentation
   - Drug controller inspections stressful
